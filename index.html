Why HopeTails Exists

"There are thousands of cats waiting. The flights already exist. The problem is connection."

Every day, rescuers around the world find animals that need a home — often in countries where adoption is near-impossible. And every day, hundreds of flights take off with empty cabin space.

HopeTails exists in that gap. We built the infrastructure to match a cat in Amman with a family in Toronto, through a traveler on that exact flight — handling every document, every milestone, every moment of the journey.

We call them Flight Parents. And they change everything.

The Process

Six stages.
One new life.

01

Stage 01

The Profile

Give the cat an identity. Photos, video, GPS tag, and a shareable adoption card that travels with them.

02

Stage 02

The Match

Confirm an adopter. The app auto-generates country-specific document requirements for the destination.

03

Stage 03

The Fund

Track vet bills and flight costs in real-time. Linked to GoFundMe or PayPal — fully transparent to the community.

04

Stage 04

The Flight Parent

A traveler posts their itinerary. A match is made. A three-way chat opens between volunteer, traveler, and adopter.

05

Stage 05

The Journey

Four live checkpoints with photo proof: Prep → Handover → Landing → Home. The whole community watches.

🔒 Requires stages 2 & 3
06

Stage 06

The Happy Tail

The profile moves to the global gallery. Rescue photo vs. new-life photo. A wall of second chances, forever.

Flights cannot be arranged until documents and funding are fully verified. Enforced by the app.

HopeTails Amman, Jordan Stage 4 of 6 — Flight Parent Needed

On a cold November morning, he appeared — perched silently on a windowsill like he’d been waiting to be found. His name became Winter, and it suited him perfectly: quiet, striking, and carrying something heavy beneath the surface.

Winter arrived with deep wounds across his back, almost certainly from a surprise attack by another stray — a cruel fate for a deaf cat who never heard it coming. Despite the pain, he purred. Despite the fear, he leaned in for cuddles.

Week 2

But Winter fought.
And Winter won.

Today, at six months old, this silky, soulful boy has made a full recovery. He loves nothing more than curling into someone’s lap — gentle, deeply loving, and entirely unaware of how extraordinary he is.
